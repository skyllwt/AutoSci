## Definition

CCSB-HI1 is the first version of the Center for Cancer Systems Biology Human Interactome dataset: 2,754 binary human protein-protein interactions over 1,549 proteins, generated by high-throughput stringent yeast two-hybrid screens of human ORFeome v1.1 (~8,100 ORFs, ~7,200 genes) and verified at ~78% rate by co-affinity purification GST pull-down in 293T cells.

## Intuition

CCSB-HI1 is a low-bias scaffold edge set for the human PPI graph. It is not comprehensive — covers ~10% of the eventual search space and ~1% of the estimated full human interactome — but is the first systematic, lab-generated, openly released human binary-PPI map.

## Formal notation

- Search space: Space-I = 7,200 x 7,200 ORFeome-v1.1 gene matrix
- |CCSB-HI1| = 2,754 binary interactions
- |V_CCSB-HI1| = 1,549 proteins (~21% of proteins tested)
- Verification rate (co-AP): ~78% on Y2H-only edges, ~81% on Y2H ∩ LCI edges
- Overlap with literature-curated subsets: 2.3% (LCI), 4.6% (LCI-core), 8.4% (LCI-hypercore)

## Variants

- **CCSB-HI1**: the original release described here (2005).
- **HI-II / HuRI / HI-union**: subsequent CCSB releases (Rolland et al. 2014, Luck et al. 2020) that expanded coverage and methodology, ultimately producing the human reference interactome (HuRI).
- **CCSB-HI1 + LCI**: union of CCSB-HI1 with the 4,067 literature-curated binary interactions in Space-I, providing the larger network analyzed in Rual et al.

## Comparison

- vs. **literature-curated (LCI) sets**: LCI inherits inspection bias toward well-studied (e.g., cancer) proteins; CCSB-HI1 is unbiased by community attention but biased by Y2H assay properties.
- vs. **AP-MS interactome maps**: AP-MS maps (e.g., BioPlex) recover stable complexes, not direct binary contacts; CCSB-HI1 explicitly targets binary edges.
- vs. **predicted interologs**: CCSB-HI1 is experimentally measured in human cells (Y2H + co-AP), not inferred by orthology.

## When to use

- As a low-inspection-bias prior for graph-based PPI prediction and validation studies.
- As a benchmark when comparing newer high-throughput assays or ML-driven PPI predictors.
- When seeding disease-PPI candidate lists for follow-up validation.

## Known limitations

- Coverage is ~1% of the estimated full human interactome — missing edges should not be interpreted as evidence of non-interaction.
- Y2H assay biases: under-representation of membrane proteins, missed transient contacts that fail to fold or localize correctly in the yeast nucleus.
- Reproducibility of any single Y2H pass is ~55%; multiple passes / orthogonal assays are needed for higher recall.
- Static snapshot — does not record context (cell type, condition, PTM state, localization).

## Open problems

- Closing the coverage gap toward the full human interactome — addressed by HuRI and successors.
- Mapping binary edges onto complex architecture and structural detail — partially addressed by structurally resolved PPI networks built on AlphaFold predictions.
- Accounting for tissue- and condition-specific PPIs.

## My understanding

CCSB-HI1 is conceptually the "v1.0" of the human binary-PPI map. Its main contribution is the methodology and the open release more than the absolute coverage; every subsequent CCSB release (HI-II, HuRI) iterated on the same pipeline. As a wiki entity it doubles as a *concept* (a named, well-bounded dataset with assay protocol) and as the entry point for tracking the lineage of human-interactome releases over time.

## Statement

Magnetar giant flares can excite high-frequency (kHz) quasi-periodic oscillations during the main burst peak through nonlinear interactions of Alfvén waves in the magnetosphere, which dissipate via reconnection on a few-millisecond timescale set by the Alfvén crossing time, t_A ~ pi*r/c ~ 1 ms.

## Evidence summary

GRB 200415A (a magnetar giant flare candidate from the direction of NGC 253) provides the first clean main-peak detection. ASIM/MXGS recorded 1-microsecond resolution data without saturation, revealing significant QPOs at ~2,132 Hz and ~4,250 Hz during the 0.8–3.2 ms peak that vanish ~3.5 ms after onset. The 0.469 ms timescale corresponds to a lengthscale of ~140 km, consistent with the magnetar's magnetosphere. The QPO disappearance coincides with the predicted termination of magnetospheric reconnection. Swift/BAT GUANO independently confirms the lower-frequency QPO. Spectral evolution (~40–60 keV blackbody, plus a ~1 MeV non-thermal component during the peak) is consistent with the fireball-plus-resonant-cyclotron-scattering picture associated with magnetospheric reconnection.

## Conditions and scope

Restricted to magnetars (B ~ 10^15 G) during giant-flare events. The mechanism predicts: (i) QPO frequencies tied to magnetospheric Alfvén timescales, (ii) finite QPO lifetime of a few milliseconds matching the reconnection duration, (iii) f2 as the first harmonic or as the signature of multi-line interactions, (iv) coincident rise of non-thermal emission. All four conditions are met for GRB 200415A.

## Counter-evidence

The data do not exclude the competing interpretation that f1 = 2,132 Hz and its overtone correspond to high-radial-order shear modes of the magneto-elastic crust. The 1,353 Hz feature significant in HED but not LED is unexplained by either model in detail. The sample is one event, so generalization to all magnetar giant flares is premature.

## Open questions

- Will future magnetar giant flares observed with similar instrumental capability (sub-microsecond timing, no saturation) reproduce the kHz main-peak QPOs?
- Can spectro-temporal joint modeling distinguish the magnetospheric-reconnection scenario from crustal-oscillation models?
- How does the population distribution of QPO frequencies map onto magnetar magnetic-field strengths and crustal compositions?

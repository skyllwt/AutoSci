## Overview

medpredict is the working umbrella for computational and machine-learning approaches that predict biomedical molecular state — protein structure, protein-protein and protein-substrate interactions, post-translational modification (PTM) sites, and drug-design-relevant features that feed targeted-therapy pipelines. The seed corpus pulls together AlphaFold-family structure prediction, the AlphaFold DB, MusiteDeep PTM-site prediction, the UbiBrowser E3-substrate interaction platform, large-scale Y2H interactome maps (CCSB-HI1), AlphaFold2-driven structurally resolved PPI networks, ubiquitin-ligase oncology, multi-omics analysis for targeted therapies, geometric deep learning on molecular representations, and PTM-targeted drug design.

## Timeline

- 2005: First proteome-scale Y2H map of binary human protein-protein interactions (CCSB-HI1).
- 2017: UbiBrowser E3-substrate prediction platform; Nature Reviews Cancer synthesis on ubiquitin ligases in oncogenesis.
- 2020: MusiteDeep PTM-site prediction webserver; PTM-isoform-targeted drug design review.
- 2021: AlphaFold2 (Jumper et al.) — first regularly-atomic-accuracy structure predictor; geometric deep learning on molecular representations review.
- 2023-2024: AlphaFold DB scales to 214M sequences; structurally resolved human PPI network using AlphaFold2; AlphaFold3 generalizes to protein-ligand / protein-NA / antibody-antigen complexes.
- 2024: Multi-omics-to-targeted-therapy synthesis review.
